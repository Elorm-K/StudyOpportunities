# Cold-email templates

Structure for a tailored cold email to a PI about a funded position. Keep it short (a busy PI scans in
seconds), specific, and evidence-backed. One PI per email — never mass-send.

## Subject
`Prospective PhD/RA — {subfield}, {intake} (funding enquiry)`
or `{subfield} PhD applicant interested in {specific paper/project}`

## Body — three short paragraphs

1. **Who + specific hook (1–2 sentences).** Name, current degree/institution, and a *specific*
   reference to the PI's work — a named paper, method, or project — showing you actually read it.
   Generic praise reads as spam.
2. **Why you fit (2–3 sentences).** The most relevant experience/result tied to their work, your
   `{subfield}` focus, and a concrete skill you'd bring. Mention any test scores / grade only if
   strong and relevant.
3. **The ask (1–2 sentences).** Are they recruiting funded PhD/RA students for `{intake}`? State your
   fee status / nationality plainly (so funding eligibility is clear upfront), and offer to send CV +
   transcript + a short research statement.

## Attachments / close
- Attach (or offer) CV; link a portfolio/Google Scholar if relevant.
- Polite close + full name + current affiliation.

## Etiquette
- Personalize every email; never CC multiple PIs.
- One concise follow-up after ~2 weeks if no reply, then stop.
- Be honest about funding need — don't hide that you need full funding for an international.
- No attachments-heavy first email if the department prefers a portal; check their page first.
