# US scholarships & funding

> US funding for internationals is mostly **structural** (institution- and department-level), not a
> handful of named national awards. The two routes that actually deliver full funding are
> **need-blind/full-need undergraduate aid** (a tiny elite group) and **funded PhD assistantships**
> (the norm in STEM). `scholarship-matching` hard-gates before ranking; `university-matching` reads
> the need-blind list below as a funding signal — **verify each school on its own page** (the list
> and effective class years shift).

## Named awards & funding routes

| Award / route | Level | Value | Nationality / eligibility gate | Merit vs need | Source |
|---------------|-------|-------|--------------------------------|---------------|--------|
| **Fulbright Foreign Student Program** | Graduate (Master's / Doctoral) + research | Monthly living stipend + health benefits (ASPE: up to $100k/illness, $25 deductible) + round-trip airfare; **may include tuition** (varies by country) | Non-US citizen; **apply through home-country Fulbright Commission / US Embassy** (rules vary by country); hold US-bachelor-equivalent; English fluency (TOEFL ~79–80 / IELTS 6.5+, country may set higher) | **Merit-based** | foreign.fulbrightonline.org |
| **Institutional merit/need aid (UG)** | Undergraduate | Institution-specific; far scarcer & more competitive than for US citizens | **No national award** — apply to each college; most US colleges are **need-aware** for internationals | Both, per institution | nafsa.org |
| **Funded PhD assistantship (RA/TA/fellowship)** | PhD (+ many STEM research master's) | **Full**: tuition waiver + living stipend + health insurance, typically guaranteed ~5 yrs | Open to internationals (subject to visa work-hour limits, ~20 hrs/wk); **awarded by department/PI, not a central scholarship office** — admission and funding are effectively one decision | **Merit/fit-based** | gradschool.cornell.edu |

## Need-blind + full-need for international undergraduates (the rare exception)

Most US colleges are **need-aware** for international applicants (ability to pay can affect
admission, aid is limited — NAFSA). A very small group admits internationals **without regard to
ability to pay** and **meets 100% of demonstrated need**. Verified on official pages (2026-06-20):

- **MIT** — need-blind, need-based, full-need for all students incl. international. (sfs.mit.edu)
- **Princeton** — need-blind for all incl. international; 100% need met with grants (no loans). (admission.princeton.edu)
- **Yale** — need-blind admissions + need-based aid for foreign citizens; full need, no loans. (admissions.yale.edu)
- **Amherst** — need-blind for internationals; meets 100% of calculated need. (amherst.edu)
- **Harvard** — need-blind for internationals; meets 100% of need. (college.harvard.edu)

> **The membership of this group shifts** — treat the count as approximate and **re-verify each
> school on its own page** before presenting (CLAUDE.md guardrail 5). Specific aid dollar amounts are
> institution-specific and intentionally not asserted here.

## Funded-PhD model (structural facts)

A fully funded PhD offer bundles **(1) full tuition coverage, (2) a living stipend, (3) health
insurance** via fellowship / RA / TA, usually guaranteed ~5 years. It is the **norm for STEM PhDs** —
students generally should not pay tuition out of pocket. Funding flows from a PI's research grants
(RA) or department teaching needs (TA), so the funding decision rides with the PI/department.
Representative figure: Cornell reports ~99% of PhD students fully funded; 2026-27 academic-year max
stipend $52,026 (varies widely by university, field, and city — **not universal**).

## Deadline windows (recurring; verify per cycle)

- **Fulbright** — set per country by the local Commission/Embassy (no single global date; roughly Feb–Oct, ~12–15 mo before US start). Check the home-country page.
- **UG admission + aid** — Early Decision/Action ~Nov 1; Regular Decision ~Jan 1–15 for fall; CSS Profile due alongside.
- **PhD** — applications ~Dec–Jan for fall; funded offers Feb–Apr; decision deadline **Apr 15** (Council of Graduate Schools convention).
